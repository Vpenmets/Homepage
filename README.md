        <h2>About iGEM</h2>
        <p>iGEM (International Genetically Engineered Machine) is a competition that challenges students from universities all around the world to solve local and global problems with synthetic biology. At the end of each year, iGEM teams travel to Paris to defend the scientific merit and community impact of their projects.</p>

        <h2>UMaryland iGEM</h2>
        <p>UMaryland iGEM is a team of undergraduate students at the University of Maryland, College Park that competes in iGEM annually. Our organization is student-run, and we work on projects related to plastic degradation, the banana plight, methane pollution, and the bioremediation of phosphorus and nitrogen in the Chesapeake Bay.</p>
